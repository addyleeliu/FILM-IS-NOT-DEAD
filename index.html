FILM IS NOT DEAD.

A Guide to Developing Your Own (B&W) Film →

Supplies (in order of use):

  • Well-ventilated area
  • Rubber gloves
  • Beakers
  • B&W developer
  • Stop bath
  • B&W fixer
  • Digital thermometer
  • Developing tank
  • Film reels
  • B&W 35mm film in its canister
  • Bottle opener
  • Scissors
  • Changing bag
  • Sink or bathtub
  • Timer
  • Wetting agent (optional)
  • Bathroom
  • Clothespins or clips

Step 1: Assemble chemicals.

  1. Use rubber gloves in a well-ventilated area to prepare beakers of your developer, stop bath, and fixer according to the manufacturer's instructions.
  2. Wait for your developer solution to come back down to room temperature (68Â°F) using your thermometer.

Step 2: Load film.

  1. Place your tank, reel, the canister with the film you want to develop, a bottle opener, and scissors inside your changing bag before securely closing the bag to ensure that no light seeps in.
  2. With your arms in the designated holes of the changing bag, use a bottle opener to remove your film from its canister.
  3. Unwind your film from the spool. When you reach the end, use scissors to cut the film from the spool.
  4. Wrap your film around the reel before positioning the reel at the center of your developing tank.
  5. After tightly shutting the lid of the developing tank, open the changing bag and remove the tank.

Step 3: Pre-wash film.

  1. To prevent the formation of air bubbles on your film, pour room temperature tap water into the designated hole in your developing tank, filling it to the brim.
  2. Shake the tank for a few seconds before disposing of the water.

Step 4: Add developer.

  1. Developer is used to convert latent images to visible images.
  2. Pour in the amount of developer solution as specified by your developing tank.
  3. Using your timer, let your film soak for the amount of time indicated by your developer's manufacturer, the producer of your film, or the Massive Dev Chart.
  4. Shake the tank as specified by the agitation routine given by the manufacturer of the developer you're using.
  5. Once time is up, dispose of the developer.

Step 5: Add stop bath.

  1. To halt further development of the film, flush the tank with your stop bath solution.
  2. Dispose of the solution under running water or pour it back into its container to be reused.

Step 6: Add fixer.

  1. To stabilize your film, fill the developing tank with fixer.
  2. Shake the tank as specified by the agitation routine given by the manufacturer of the fixer you're using.
  3. Dispose of the solution under running water or pour it back into its container to be reused.

Step 7: Rinse.

  1. Rinse the tank with running tap water for a few minutes.
  2. If you choose, wetting agent can now be added as an optional step to reduce the chance of getting water spots on your film.

Step 8: Dry film.

  1. Use clothespins or clips to hang your film up for drying in a moist, dust-free environment such as a bathroom.
  2. Let your film dry fully. This usually takes between two and five hours.
  3. Admire the black and white film you developed yourself!
  4. You can now choose to scan or enlarge your developed film in order to edit or make prints of your images.
